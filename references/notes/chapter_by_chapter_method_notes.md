# Chapter-by-Chapter Method Notes

## Overall structure
The book is most useful when treated as a pipeline:
1. identify the first structural impulse
2. measure price, time, and slope
3. transform the impulse into lines, ratios, and levels
4. look for time-price intersections
5. wait for market confirmation

## Lines
- measured move is the base vector
- first impulse sets the native axis

## Logarithms
- use log space for percentage-consistent structure
- geometric mean matters on log charts

## JTTL
- one of the most explicit constructs in the book
- use a structurally important origin
- project root-based targets forward in time

## Squaring the range
- convert price and range into time-count candidates
- row-count logic matters

## Circles / Squares
- geometric transforms of the impulse
- must be normalized mathematically in code

## Ratios / Pythagorean / Music
- treat as candidate transform families
- keep experimental until validated

## Let the market tell you
- projection is not entry
- confirmation is required
